# {{title}}

**TL;DR:** _One to two sentences. What is it, what does it do for the customer, and what's the headline result._

## The Problem

_What pain exists, for whom, and how do we know it's real? Ground it with evidence: customer feedback, support data, observed failure modes. Include a concrete example that makes the pain visceral._

## Why Now

_What has changed that makes this worth doing now? Market shift, dependency unblocked, customer escalation, strategic window. If this section is hard to write, question whether the timing is right._

## What We Propose

_High-level direction at the benefit level. What will the customer experience? How does it work conceptually (not architecturally)? Keep this the shortest section. Two to three paragraphs max._

## What This Is Not

_Explicit boundaries. What are we deliberately excluding? What adjacent problems are we not solving? Prevents misalignment before it starts._

-

## Key Results

_If you have prototype, pilot, or benchmark data, present the headline number with enough context to be credible. One table or chart max. Link to the full analysis._

## Tradeoffs

_What does this cost? Latency, compute, complexity, opportunity cost. Be honest. A one-pager that hides risk loses trust._

-

## Next Steps

_What needs to happen to move forward? Decision needed, resources required, timeline (milestones only, not a project plan)._

-

## Links

_Pointers to deeper material: repo, spec draft, research, benchmark data, related decisions._
